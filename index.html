I was born in Valdivia, Chile in 1992. I received my B.Sc. degree, professional title in Computer Science and a M.Sc. in Informatics (High-Performance Computing and Data Science) from the Austral University of Chile. Today, I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computational Neuroscience at the Ernst Strüngmann Institute (ESI) for Neuroscience in Cooperation with Max Planck Society (Frankfurt, Germany) and the Donders Centre for Neuroscience at Radboud University, Nijmegen, Netherlands.

My research interests lie in the development and application of computational methods to analyse multi-neuron spiking patterns focus on the precise timing of neuronal spike sequences. In addition to my fascination with computational neuroscience, I am also very passionate about High-Performance Computing, Data Visualization, Graph Theory, Network Science, and Education.

When it comes to hobbies, music holds a special place in my life, and I enjoy playing the guitar during my leisure moments. Also, I have some experience playing bass and drums. Exploring the outdoors, immersing myself in books, engaging in (video)games, and deep conversations also bring me great joy. I get a bit loco in metal-hxc concerts.
